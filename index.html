Conference Starts In:

Quality Skills Development and Apprenticeship

“Transforming Apprenticeship Systems in the Informal Economy for Youth Employability in Africa”

About the Conference

The Regional Conference on Quality Skills Development and Apprenticeships, will be held from 14th to 16th May 2025 in Mombasa, Kenya. Held under the theme ‘Transforming Apprenticeship Systems in the Informal Economy in Africa’, the conference is designed to advance and implement actions and commitments on transitioning from the informal to formal economic and apprenticeship systems. The conference serves as a critical forum for discussing the informality agenda within the context of sustainable development priorities. The conference will host delegates from various African countries including representatives from the informal sector, SASASNET officials, representatives from development agencies, and international governmental and non-governmental organizations.

Conference Themes

  • Innovative models of apprenticeship for informal sector skills development
  • Strengthening policy frameworks to support apprenticeships
  • Financing and incentivizing apprenticeship systems
  • Gender and inclusion in informal economy training programs

Programs & Activities

The conference features keynotes, panel discussions, exhibitions, workshops, and networking.

Conference Information

Conference Venue

The conference will be held at Mombasa Continental Resort, Mombasa.

Visa and Health Requirements

VISA REQUIREMENTS: Participants/delegates must obtain an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) before commencing their journey to Kenya. eTA applications for Kenya are usually processed in 3 days, with an urgent processing service available. However, it is recommended that applications be submitted at least 2 weeks prior to travel to ensure adequate time for processing.

Persons exempt from obtaining the eTA:

  • Holders of valid Kenya Passports or one-way Emergency Certificates issued by Kenya Missions abroad.
  • Holders of Kenya Permanent Residence, valid Work Permits, and Passes.
  • Holders of valid United Nations Conventional Travel Documents issued by the Government of Kenya.
  • Members of Diplomatic Missions and International Organizations Accredited to Kenya.
  • Citizens of the East African Partner States: Burundi, DRC, Rwanda, South Sudan, Tanzania, and Uganda.

For more information, please see: www.etakenya.go.ke

Health Requirements

All travellers arriving in Kenya should present a valid yellow fever certificate. For more details see: Health Requirements

Participants and delegates will be provided with local transportation between the airport and the conference venue. Additionally, it is recommended that participants and delegates download and use taxi apps for convenient transport services within Kenya.
